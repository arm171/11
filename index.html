Arman Vermishyan

Former football player and future frontend developer

About Me

My name is Arman Vermishyan. For most of my life, I was a football player — not just by skill, but by heart. Football was my world, my discipline, my identity. But life doesn’t always follow the path we choose.

After multiple injuries and three serious surgeries, I had to say goodbye to the only life I ever knew. I lost football, but I didn’t lose myself. Instead, I started building something new.

Today, I’m walking a new path — frontend development. I still have that fire in me. The same hunger to grow, to push myself, and to become something more.

I believe in hard work, in learning every day, and in using the pain of the past as fuel for the future.

What I’ve Learned in the Last 3 Years

These years have been the hardest of my life — not just physically, but emotionally.

After surgery, when your body is weak, and your mind is full of questions, you learn to wait.
You learn to accept silence.
You learn to live one day at a time.

I taught myself how to do recovery exercises. I pushed myself to walk again. I faced moments of fear and doubt, but I never stopped believing that I would find a new purpose.

And I did — I discovered coding. It became my new field, my new discipline. I may not run on a football pitch anymore, but I keep moving — one line of code at a time.

My Personal Achievements

I used to wear a football jersey, now I wear responsibility.

In sport, I earned respect — not because I scored the most goals, but because of who I was on and off the field. Even rivals respected me, because I gave my heart to the game.

In life, my greatest achievement is simple: I never gave up.
Even when I felt lost, even when I had to rebuild myself from zero — I kept going.

That’s who I am — not the strongest, not the fastest, but someone who never stops.

Projects & Practice

I’m still learning. But even at the start of my journey, I’ve already built things with meaning:

  • A football player card, because football is still in my soul
  • A recipe page, because rebuilding life is also about routine and care
  • A personal portfolio, because I believe my future has a place on the web

I don't rush. I build slowly, like healing bones. But I build with honesty and passion. This is only the beginning.
